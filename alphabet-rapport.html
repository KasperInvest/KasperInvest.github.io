01
Investment Thesis

We initiate coverage of Alphabet Inc. (GOOGL) with a BUY rating and a 12-month price target of USD 400, implying 33% upside from the current USD 301. Alphabet is the most undervalued mega-cap technology company in the world. The market is applying a newspaper-industry multiple to a business that holds monopoly positions in search, the world's largest video platform, the third-largest cloud provider, and an autonomous vehicle franchise worth hundreds of billions — all at 28x forward earnings.

Pillar I — Search monopoly is intact

Google holds 89%+ global search market share — unchanged since ChatGPT launched in 2022. AI Overviews now reaches 1.5 billion monthly users and has increased query volume. The AI threat to search is real but structurally overpriced in the current valuation.

Pillar II — Cloud is the growth engine

Google Cloud grew 48% in Q4 2025 to $17.7B — the fastest growth rate among the three hyperscalers. The $240B customer backlog (up 2x YoY) is contracted revenue waiting to be recognised. Cloud operating income surged 154% to $5.3B.

Pillar III — YouTube is undervalued

YouTube exceeded $60B in combined ads and subscriptions revenue in 2025 — surpassing Netflix's total revenue. It is the #1 streaming platform in the US by watch time. As a standalone company, YouTube would command a $400–600B valuation.

Pillar IV — Waymo is a free option

Waymo is seeking to raise $15B at a $100–110B valuation while operating at minimal cost to Alphabet shareholders. CEO Pichai called Waymo 'meaningful in our financials' by 2027-28. This optionality is essentially unpriced in current multiples.

Pillar V — Capital return is massive

Alphabet has a net cash position of ~$95B and bought back ~$70B of stock in 2025. The board declared a 5% dividend increase in Q1 2025. At current prices, the combined buyback yield exceeds 5% annually — extraordinary for a company growing revenue at 15–18%.

Pillar VI — AI is a tailwind, not a headwind

Gemini AI has 750M+ monthly active users. AI Overviews has increased search engagement. Apple has signed a deal to lease Google data centers for Siri AI — a direct validation of Google's AI infrastructure quality. The narrative of AI destroying Google is empirically wrong.

02
Company Overview

Alphabet Inc. was formed in 2015 as the holding company for Google and its various subsidiaries. Headquartered in Mountain View, California, Alphabet operates through three reporting segments: Google Services, Google Cloud, and Other Bets. The company is listed on Nasdaq under the tickers GOOGL (Class A, with voting rights) and GOOG (Class C, no voting rights). Co-founders Larry Page and Sergey Brin retain supermajority voting control through Class B shares.

Google Services — the core business — generated $346B of the $402B in FY25 revenue and includes Search & other (the advertising monopoly), YouTube ads and subscriptions, Android, Chrome, Google Play, and Google One cloud storage subscriptions (now 270M+ paid subscribers). This segment operates at a ~42% operating margin, generating extraordinary free cash flow.

Google Cloud is the growth engine, contributing approximately $54B in FY25 revenue (growing at 40%+ annualised). It encompasses Google Cloud Platform (GCP) infrastructure, enterprise AI solutions, and Workspace (formerly G Suite). The segment reached a 30%+ operating margin in Q4 2025, having been loss-making as recently as 2022. Other Bets includes Waymo (autonomous vehicles), DeepMind, and other long-duration bets.

03
Industry Analysis

Alphabet operates across three of the most structurally attractive markets in the global economy: digital advertising (~$750B TAM growing at 10%+ annually), cloud infrastructure (~$600B TAM growing at 20%+ annually), and AI services (TAM currently undefined but likely the largest long-term opportunity in technology). The company's unique position is that it is simultaneously a beneficiary of, and a key infrastructure provider for, the AI revolution.

The dominant investor concern — that AI-native search (ChatGPT, Perplexity) will erode Google's search advertising revenue — is empirically unsupported after three years of evidence. Google's search market share has remained above 89% globally. More importantly, AI Overviews has materially increased user engagement rather than reducing it. Search revenue grew 17% in Q4 2025. The fear is real; the revenue impact is not.

In cloud, Alphabet competes with AWS (Amazon) and Azure (Microsoft). Google Cloud is the fastest-growing of the three hyperscalers at 48% YoY in Q4 2025, versus AWS at approximately 19% and Azure at approximately 31%. Google's AI-infrastructure differentiation — built on TPUs (Tensor Processing Units) developed internally — has become a genuine competitive moat in the enterprise AI workload market, where demand significantly exceeds supply.

04
Growth Drivers

Google Cloud backlog conversion is the most visible near-term driver. The $240B customer backlog — up 2x year-over-year — represents contracted revenue at an average conversion period of 12–24 months. If Cloud grows 45–50% in 2026 (reaching ~$75–80B), it will contribute roughly 15% of Alphabet's total revenue at a 30%+ operating margin, significantly lifting consolidated profitability and driving EPS above current consensus estimates.

YouTube's subscription business is an underappreciated structural growth driver. YouTube TV, YouTube Premium, and YouTube Music now contribute meaningfully to the $60B+ YouTube revenue base. The platform surpassed Netflix in US watch time in 2025 — and unlike Netflix, YouTube generates the majority of its content for free through its creator economy. The subscription layer represents high-margin, low-churn recurring revenue with significant expansion runway internationally.

Waymo represents the largest optionality in the Alphabet portfolio. At a $100–110B valuation in its latest financing round and targeting 1M weekly rides by late 2026 at a $20 average fare, Waymo is on the cusp of generating $1B+ in annualised revenue. This is tiny relative to Alphabet's scale today — but Waymo is potentially the most valuable autonomous vehicle franchise globally, and it is carried at essentially zero value in Alphabet's current stock price.

05
Financial Performance

Alphabet's FY2025 results were exceptional across the board. Q4 2025 revenue came in at $113.8B (+18% YoY), beating consensus estimates. Full-year 2025 revenue is estimated at approximately $402B (+15% YoY). The company faces one genuine near-term headwind: capital expenditure compression of free cash flow. Alphabet guided $175–185B in CapEx for 2026, nearly double 2025's ~$93B, as it builds AI infrastructure to capture the $240B cloud backlog.

The CapEx surge is the primary bear argument for 2026. FCF is expected to decline year-over-year as depreciation catches up with infrastructure spend. This is a temporary earnings headwind — not a structural margin deterioration. The infrastructure being built today will generate Cloud revenue through 2028–2031, with operating margins expanding as the fixed-cost base is absorbed.

Valuation

At USD 301, Alphabet trades at approximately 28x FY26E earnings and 23x FY27E earnings — a significant discount to its five-year average P/E of approximately 25–30x, and at or below the S&P 500's forward P/E. The 60-analyst consensus has zero sell ratings and an average 12-month price target of USD 377, implying 25% upside from consensus alone. Our target of USD 400 is modestly above consensus, reflecting higher Cloud growth assumptions and partial Waymo optionality.

07
Key Risks
Antitrust / Forced Divestiture

US and EU regulators have found Google guilty of maintaining illegal search and ad-tech monopolies. Remedies could include forced default-search contract bans, advertising business separation, or structural breakup. This is the most severe tail risk.

CapEx Overspend / AI Demand Miss

Alphabet is committing $175–185B in 2026 CapEx assuming AI demand will fill the capacity. If AI enterprise adoption slows, Google could be left with costly underutilised infrastructure, compressing FCF for years.

Search Disruption Acceleration

Despite three years of resilience, search disruption risk is deferred, not eliminated. A step-change improvement from OpenAI, Perplexity, or Apple Intelligence could accelerate switching. This risk grows over a 3–5 year horizon.

Macro / Ad Cycle Downturn

~56% of Alphabet's revenue is still advertising. A recession would directly impact Search and YouTube revenue. The company's revenue is more cyclical than its franchise quality suggests, as demonstrated in 2022.

08
Near-Term Catalysts
Q1 2026 Cloud Beat

If Google Cloud sustains 45%+ growth vs. ~35% consensus in Q1 2026, it would trigger significant upward EPS revisions and a multiple re-rating. This is the single most important near-term catalyst.

Waymo Financing / Spin-off Announcement

The $15B Waymo fundraising round at $100–110B valuation would force the market to recognise Waymo's value. Any spin-off announcement would unlock additional multiple expansion.

Apple AI Partnership Expansion

Apple already leases Google data centers for Siri AI. Further expansion of this partnership would validate Google's AI infrastructure and generate incremental high-margin Cloud revenue.

Antitrust Resolution

A lighter-than-expected antitrust remedy — particularly avoiding forced divestiture — would remove a significant overhang and drive a re-rating toward intrinsic value.

09
Conclusion

Alphabet is the world's most undervalued mega-cap technology company. The market is pricing in a slow, steady decline in search dominance that three years of evidence has not delivered. Meanwhile, Google Cloud is growing at 48% — faster than AWS or Azure — with a $240B backlog that guarantees multi-year revenue acceleration. YouTube is the world's largest video platform by watch time. Waymo is a free option worth potentially $100–110B alone.

The legitimate bear case — CapEx-driven FCF compression in 2026 and antitrust regulatory risk — is real but temporary and binary respectively. FCF will recover in 2027–2028 as infrastructure depreciation stabilises.

We initiate coverage with a BUY rating and a USD 400 price target (33% upside). The 60-analyst consensus with zero sell ratings and a $377 average target confirms the fundamental quality. The gap between intrinsic value and current price reflects temporary fear — not permanent impairment.

The core insight: Alphabet is not a newspaper company. It is a four-business conglomerate — each worth hundreds of billions individually — being priced as though AI will render search obsolete within 24 months. It won't. And when the market recognises this, the re-rating will be swift.
